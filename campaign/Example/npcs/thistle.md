# Thistle

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Scout and messenger for the Circle of Roots
- **Location:** Often found roaming the edges of the Elder Oak grove or spying on Newforge
- **Stat Block:** Druid (CR 2)
- **Faction:** The Circle of Roots

## Appearance
Thistle is a small, wiry gnome with skin the color of damp earth and hair that resembles a patch of wild, unkempt moss. They wear simple tunics made of woven grass and bark, and they are always barefoot. Their eyes are a bright, startling amber, like a fox's.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks in quick, clipped sentences, often pausing to tilt their head as if listening to something the party can't hear.
- **Physical Tell:** Scratches their forearm when nervous or lying; has a habit of squatting on chairs rather than sitting in them.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To find out what the "Metal-Men" (Iron Axemen) are planning for the next moon cycle.
- **Want (deep):** To prove themselves to High Druid Elara and be granted the title of "Leaf-Speaker."
- **Secret:** Thistle once snuck into Newforge and tasted "ale" and "bread," and secretly finds human food much more interesting than the berries and roots they usually eat.

## Relationships
- **High Druid Elara:** Their mentor and leader; Thistle is intensely loyal but also somewhat intimidated by her.
- **Kaelen:** A ranger they sometimes cooperate with to steer travelers away from dangerous parts of the forest.
- **Sergeant Brak:** A member of the Axemen who once caught Thistle spying; Brak let them go with a warning, and Thistle is now strangely fascinated by him.

## Sample Dialogue
> "Trees are screaming. Can't you hear it? No, of course not. Too much metal on you. Clank, clank, clank."
> "Newforge is a scab. A big, grey, smoky scab on the valley's skin. We should pick it off."
> "I can show you the path. But if you touch a single sapling with that blade, the roots will have you before you can blink."

## DM Notes
Thistle is the party's most likely first contact with the Circle of Roots. They should be played as slightly feral and very twitchy. They aren't inherently hostile, but they are deeply prejudiced against anything industrial. 

Use Thistle to provide lore about the forest and the blight. They can act as a guide through the more treacherous parts of the Oakhaven Valley, but they will constantly criticize the party's equipment and "heavy feet."
