# World — Curse of Strahd

## Campaign Overview
- **Adventure:** Curse of Strahd
- **System:** D&D 5e
- **World:** Ravenloft / Domains of Dread
- **Region:** Barovia — a demiplane sealed within perpetual mists
- **Tone:** Gothic horror. Despair, imprisonment, obsession, and the faint hope of redemption. Dark and oppressive, but not gratuitous. Players should feel the weight of the land.
- **Themes:** Fate vs. free will, love and obsession, the corruption of power, light in overwhelming darkness

---

## The Land of Barovia

Barovia is not a place on any normal map. It is a **demiplane** — a pocket realm created and sustained by the Dark Powers as a prison for Strahd von Zarovich. The mists that surround it are impassable. Those who wander into the mists find themselves walking back into Barovia, or simply vanishing. There is no escape unless Strahd is destroyed — and even then, it is not guaranteed.

**Key truths about Barovia:**
- The land itself is cursed. Crops fail. Weather is cold and grey. The sun rarely breaks through the clouds, and when it does, it feels like an intrusion.
- Strahd has ruled for over 400 years. Barovians are beaten down, superstitious, and fatalistic. Most have given up.
- **Resurrection magic does not work reliably in Barovia.** The Dark Powers interfere. Raise Dead and similar spells may fail or have complications.
- Strahd is aware of notable strangers in his land. He watches. He tests. He is not a brute — he is a strategist, an aristocrat, and a showman.
- The Tarokka reading (by Madam Eva) determines the locations of three artifacts and an ally that can help defeat Strahd. These locations are randomized per campaign.

---

## History (Brief)

Strahd von Zarovich was a great military general who conquered the valley of Barovia for his family. He fell in love with **Tatyana**, a young woman who loved his younger brother Sergei instead. On the day of Sergei's wedding, Strahd made a pact with the Dark Powers, murdered his brother, and drank his blood — becoming the first vampire. Tatyana fled and threw herself from the walls of Castle Ravenloft rather than be taken. She has been reincarnated many times since. The current incarnation is **Ireena Kolyana**.

Strahd has ruled Barovia ever since, trapped in his demiplane, alternately tormenting his subjects and obsessively pursuing whatever woman carries Tatyana's soul.

---

## The People of Barovia

Barovians are insular, superstitious, and largely hopeless. They know they are trapped. Most lock their doors at night, avoid the roads after dark, and have made an uneasy peace with their situation.

- **Do not travel at night** — the roads belong to Strahd's creatures after dark.
- **Children are protected instinctively** — adults will do almost anything to keep children safe.
- **The Morninglord is largely a dead faith** — the clergy exists but its god feels absent. Some cling to it desperately; others have abandoned it.
- Barovians speak with a slightly archaic, Eastern European-flavored cadence. Formal address is common with strangers.

---

## Current Situation

The party has entered Barovia through the mists. Madam Eva has read the Tarokka cards. The artifacts and ally locations are known. Strahd is aware of the party and is actively monitoring them — alternately curious, amused, and calculating.

Key threads:
- **Ireena Kolyana** must be protected or Strahd will take her
- **The three artifacts** (Holy Symbol of Ravenkind, Sunsword, Tome of Strahd) must be found
- **An ally** (determined by Tarokka) will help in the final confrontation
- **Castle Ravenloft** is the endgame — Strahd must be destroyed in his lair
