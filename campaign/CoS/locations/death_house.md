# Death House

## At a Glance
- **Type:** Haunted manor; introductory dungeon; cult death trap
- **Region:** Village of Barovia, at the edge of the fog on the main road
- **Tone:** Eerie, then horrifying, then tragic — in that order
- **Scale:** Large three-story townhouse with a sub-basement dungeon complex beneath
- **Controlled by:** The spirits of the cult that built it; the house itself, which has a will of its own

## Read-Aloud Description
Two children stand in the road ahead — a girl of perhaps ten and a younger boy, maybe eight — their clothes fine but old-fashioned, their faces pale and frightened. They are crying, not loudly, just the quiet persistent crying of children who have been afraid for a very long time. Behind them, a tall Gothic manor rises four stories against the dark sky, its windows dark except for a single light in an upper room where, the girl says, a monster has been keeping their parents. The house is beautiful in the way old, serious wealth can be beautiful — wrought iron, carved stone lintels, a heavy oak door — and the closer you get, the more you notice the details that are slightly wrong: the iron fence shaped like screaming faces if you look at it sideways, the way the light in the upper window doesn't flicker like a candle but pulses, slow and rhythmic, like breathing. The girl reaches out and takes your hand, and her fingers are very cold.

## DM-Only Notes
Rose (Rosavalda) and Thorn (Thornboldt) Durst are ghosts. They died of starvation, locked in a hidden room in the attic by parents who were consumed by cult madness and forgot them. They do not know they are dead. They believe they are children asking for help. If the party discovers this, handle it with care — it is the emotional core of the location and should land with weight.

The cult that built and occupied this house worshipped Strahd, seeking his dark blessing by performing sacrifices in the basement shrine. Strahd accepted nothing from them — he found them pathetic — and they eventually destroyed themselves. The house has absorbed decades of ritual violence and grief and now perpetuates it, luring travelers in and doing its best to kill them on the altar below.

The basement contains a summoning chamber where the party must either make a sacrifice or fight a shadow demon that the house conjures. If they refuse the sacrifice (they should), the house tries to kill them as they escape. The escape sequence — the house sealing doors, animating furniture, filling with spectral wind — is the climax.

Rose and Thorn's ghosts can possess willing party members. Let it happen if players consent — it's a great roleplaying beat and the children mean no harm.

## Key Features
- **The Children (Rose and Thorn):** The hook and the heart of the location; their backstory is in the locked attic nursery
- **The Upper Floors:** Family quarters, increasingly disturbed; journals from Mr. and Mrs. Durst document the slide into cult madness
- **The Secret Staircase:** Behind a bookshelf; leads to the sub-basement dungeon where the cult did its work
- **The Shrine Room:** An ancient altar to dark power; requires a sacrifice to appease; the party should not appease it
- **The Summoning Chamber:** Where the monster is conjured if the party refuses the sacrifice; cramped, flooded, terrifying

## Occupants
- Rose and Thorn (ghosts): Do not know they are dead; want to help; will be devastated if told the truth, and that devastation is the correct emotional note to end on
- Animated armor and furnishings: The house's active defenses, dormant until provoked
- Shadows and specters: The remnants of cult members who died here; hostile
- The shambling mound (or shadow demon variant): The monster in the basement; the house's final test

## Connections
- Village of Barovia: Directly adjacent; the party almost certainly arrives here on their first day
- Castle Ravenloft: The cult worshipped Strahd; the house is a distorted echo of the castle's own darkness — smaller, sadder, built by people who didn't understand what they were reaching toward

## Atmosphere & Senses
- **Sound:** The house is too quiet at first — no settling creaks, no wind through gaps — and then, deeper in, the sound of a lullaby being hummed from a room with no one in it
- **Smell:** Dust, old wax, and beneath both: something sweet and wrong, like flowers left in a sealed room for months
- **Temperature:** Cold throughout, growing colder as you descend; the basement dungeon is near-freezing, and the air there has a quality that makes your breath visible even when the temperature shouldn't quite warrant it
