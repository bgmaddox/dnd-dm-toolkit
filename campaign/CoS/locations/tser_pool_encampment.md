# Tser Pool Encampment

## At a Glance
- **Type:** Vistani encampment; tarokka reading site; uneasy refuge
- **Region:** Eastern Barovia, on the shores of Tser Pool in the mountain foothills east of the village
- **Tone:** Warm, lively, and deeply unsettling — joy in a land that has forgotten how to produce it
- **Scale:** A cluster of eight to ten painted wagons around a large central fire on a lakeshore
- **Controlled by:** Madam Eva

## Read-Aloud Description
The smell of woodsmoke and roasting meat reaches you before the light does — and then the firelight breaks through the trees, amber and generous, and with it comes music: a fiddle playing something fast and sad at the same time, and the sound of laughter that does not feel performed. The Vistani camp sits at the edge of a black lake, its colored wagons arranged in a loose circle around a fire large enough to push back the dark, and the people around it are eating, drinking, and talking with an ease that seems impossible in this country. A woman waves you over without hesitation and presses a cup of something warm into your hands before you've said a word. It is only when you turn toward the lake that the warmth falters — the water is flat and absolutely still, cold-looking even against the firelight, and something in its depth catches the light in a way that water shouldn't, as if the reflection is not quite right.

## DM-Only Notes
The Vistani here are genuinely hospitable — they will feed the party, share wine, offer entertainment, and trade fairly. They are not a trap. They are also, some of them, in indirect service to Strahd: the Vistani of Barovia exist in a complicated arrangement with him, protected by his word in exchange for certain courtesies. Information passes. This does not make them villains, but it means the party's presence here will be noted.

Madam Eva is the dominant presence and the reason the party must come here. She reads the tarokka — the card reading that determines the locations of the three items the party needs (the holy symbol, the magical weapon, the tome) and the location of Strahd himself for the final confrontation. This reading is the structural spine of the entire campaign. Play Eva as genuinely ancient and genuinely strange: she has seen thousands of readings and she knows how this one goes, in broad strokes, without knowing the specific players. She is neither ally nor enemy. She is something older and more neutral than either.

Tser Pool itself: something large lives in it. It has not bothered anyone in years. The Vistani know it is there and they know not to swim at night.

## Key Features
- **The Central Fire:** The social heart of the camp; the party can eat, drink, and hear stories here; a good place to gather rumors and ground the players in Barovian lore
- **Madam Eva's Wagon:** The largest, most ornate wagon; she will invite the party inside for the tarokka reading; the interior is close and candlelit and smells of incense and old wood
- **The Lake Shore:** Cold, still, wrong; the surface is a perfect mirror except that it sometimes reflects things that are not there
- **The Other Wagons:** Vistani families, some willing to talk, some not; one older man will tell the party the history of Strahd if they ask and listen long enough

## Occupants
- Madam Eva: Ancient Vistana seer, possibly something more than human; performs the tarokka reading; speaks in layered truths
- Vistani families (various): Hospitable, guarded about certain topics, loyal to their own above all else
- Whatever is in the lake: Unknown; large; patient

## Connections
- Village of Barovia: A few hours west along the Svalich Road; the party's likely last stop before or first stop after the village
- Old Svalich Road: The main route continues north and west toward Vallaki from here
- Castle Ravenloft: Visible in the far distance from the lakeshore on a clear night — which is rare

## Atmosphere & Senses
- **Sound:** Fiddle music, laughter, crackling fire — and underneath, if you stand near the water, a very low, slow sound from the lake that might be current and might not be
- **Smell:** Woodsmoke, roasted meat, pine resin, and the cold mineral smell of the lake; inside Eva's wagon, incense and something faintly sweet and old
- **Temperature:** Warmer than anywhere else in Barovia by virtue of the fire; step ten feet toward the lake and the warmth disappears entirely
