# Village of Barovia

## At a Glance
- **Type:** Cursed village; entry point to the domain
- **Region:** Eastern Barovia, base of the valley
- **Tone:** Eerie, abandoned, suffocating
- **Scale:** Small village, perhaps two dozen occupied structures among many more boarded or collapsed
- **Controlled by:** Strahd von Zarovich (by terror and obsession)

## Read-Aloud Description
A thick grey fog rolls between the buildings here, swallowing the lower halves of structures and muffling all sound to a strange, cottony silence. Most of the houses are dark — shutters nailed shut, doors barred, no smoke rising from chimneys that have gone cold. The few lights visible are thin and uncertain, candlelight behind heavy curtains, and the people who made them are not outside. The dirt road through the center of town is empty, and the only movement is the slow drift of mist and, somewhere deeper in the village, the sound of a bell that stopped working years ago still somehow sounding once, faint and distant, before the silence returns. At the far end of the road, a mansion stands apart from the others, its windows lit with the amber glow of a house still being lived in — which makes it the most unnerving thing in sight.

## DM-Only Notes
Strahd has fixated on this village specifically because Ireena Kolyana lives here — she is the latest reincarnation of Tatyana, his centuries-lost love, and he has been visiting the village and stalking her for months. The burgomaster, Kolyan Indirovich, died of grief and fear. The villagers know something is targeting their neighbor and they are too broken and too afraid to do anything about it. The church has been compromised — Father Donavich's son Doru went to Castle Ravenloft with a group of adventurers and came back as a vampire spawn, locked in the church basement. Father Donavich has not slept or eaten properly in weeks. The Blood of the Vine tavern is the last thin pretense of normalcy in the village. Arik the barkeep serves wine mechanically, barely present. Three Vistani — agents of Strahd — watch from the corner and report everything to their master.

## Key Features
- **Blood of the Vine Tavern:** The only public gathering place still functioning; dim, half-empty, serves as the party's first real social hub in Barovia
- **The Church of the Morning Lord:** Structurally damaged, spiritually embattled; Father Donavich barely holding on; Doru locked and suffering below
- **Burgomaster's Mansion:** Where Ireena and Ismark live, watching over their father's body, waiting for help they had no reason to expect would come
- **Boarded Houses:** Dozens of homes sealed from within; some inhabitants are alive and hiding; some are not

## Occupants
- Ismark Kolyanovich: The burgomaster's son, capable and ashamed he couldn't protect his family; will beg the party for help moving Ireena
- Ireena Kolyana: Strahd's obsession; composed, armed, and unwilling to be treated as a prize — she has her own will and deserves to be played that way
- Father Donavich: Wrecked priest, still faithful in a hollow way; holds the key to the church basement; his mercy and his fear are at war
- Doru (vampire spawn): Donavich's son in the basement; can be spoken to, even reasoned with briefly; destroyed or helped depending on the party's choices
- Arik: Barkeep at Blood of the Vine; near-catatonic; will refill your glass

## Connections
- Castle Ravenloft: Visible to the northeast; Strahd looks down on this village from there and always has
- Tser Pool Encampment: A few hours east along the Svalich Road; the Vistani camp and Madam Eva
- Old Svalich Road: The only road in or out, fog-bound in both directions

## Atmosphere & Senses
- **Sound:** Profound silence broken only by the wind, the occasional creak of an unoiled shutter, and — rarely — a child crying somewhere indoors before being quickly hushed
- **Smell:** Damp earth, wood rot, the faint sweetness of something organic decaying under the fog
- **Temperature:** Cold and damp regardless of season; a chill that feels less like weather and more like the absence of something warm that used to be here
