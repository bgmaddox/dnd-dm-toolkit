# Town of Vallaki

## At a Glance
- **Type:** Walled town; mid-campaign hub; powder keg
- **Region:** Central Barovia, on the shores of Lake Zarovich
- **Tone:** Falsely cheerful, visibly brittle, and building toward explosion
- **Scale:** Small walled town; perhaps three hundred residents; the largest human settlement in Barovia
- **Controlled by:** Baron Vargas Vallakovich (in theory); contested by Lady Fiona Wachter (in practice, increasingly)

## Read-Aloud Description
The walls of Vallaki are solid and well-maintained, which is the first surprise — someone here still believes in defense. The gates are guarded and closed, and the guards want to know your business before they let you through. Inside, the town is busy in a way that feels almost normal until you look closer: colored paper streamers hang between the buildings, festival decorations from an event that ended days ago and has not yet been taken down because another one is already being planned, and the people moving through the market square are smiling in a way that does not involve their eyes. Banners hang from the town hall reading ALL WILL BE WELL, and they look recently painted, and there are a lot of them. A man in the square is laughing a little too hard at something, and the people around him laugh too, quickly, as if on cue, and then everyone moves on.

## DM-Only Notes
Baron Vallakovich is delusional and dangerous. He has constructed a theology of enforced optimism: if the people of Vallaki genuinely achieve happiness, Strahd's curse will be broken. He holds mandatory weekly festivals with names like "Festival of the Blazing Sun" and "Wolf's Head Jamboree." Attendance is required. Displays of unhappiness are punishable — offenders are put in stocks and pelted with rotten food or, for repeat offenses, worse. He is not unintelligent; he is wrong in a way that has become load-bearing to his identity and he cannot afford to examine it.

Lady Wachter is quietly, methodically organizing a coup. She is pragmatic to the point of amorality — she believes the only path to safety is accommodation with Strahd, and she has been cultivating her position for years. She is not a villain in the simple sense; she is a woman who has made a calculation that the party will find repugnant and that has a certain terrible logic to it.

The Blue Water Inn is run by Urwin Martikov, who is also a wereraven and a high-ranking member of the Keepers of the Feather — the secret wereraven network that is one of Barovia's few actual resistance organizations. He is one of the party's best long-term allies if they figure this out. His wife Danika runs the inn floor; their sons spy for them.

St. Andral's Church offers consecrated ground (bones of Saint Andral under the floor), making it one of the few places in Barovia where vampires cannot enter. The bones can be stolen — and someone will try.

## Key Features
- **The Town Gates:** Always closed; guards who ask questions; the Baron has ordered that all newcomers be monitored
- **Blue Water Inn:** The party's best base of operations in Vallaki; Urwin and Danika Martikov, excellent wine, secret network of wereravens
- **Town Hall:** The Baron's seat; festival planning happens here constantly; his advisor Izek Strazni (a brute with a devil's arm) enforces compliance
- **St. Andral's Church:** Father Lucian; consecrated ground; the bones of Saint Andral; a target
- **Wachterhaus:** Lady Fiona Wachter's manor; Book Club meetings that are actually cult gatherings; information and danger in equal measure

## Occupants
- Baron Vargas Vallakovich: Ruler, delusional, genuinely believes he is saving his people; will arrest anyone who speaks against him
- Izek Strazni: The Baron's enforcer; fiend-touched; obsessively collects dolls resembling Ireena (which is a whole thing)
- Lady Fiona Wachter: Measured, intelligent, dangerous; devil-cult ties; will offer to help the party if it serves her plan
- Urwin and Danika Martikov: Innkeepers, wereravens, Keepers of the Feather; potential allies and information brokers
- Father Lucian Petrovich: Priest of St. Andral's; frightened but faithful; does not know the bones are already gone if that plot is in motion

## Connections
- Village of Barovia: A half-day's travel south and east along the Svalich Road
- Krezk: A day's travel west through the mountains; requires the Baron's permission to pass (or a workaround)
- Lake Zarovich: Just north of the walls; fishing boats, cold water, the Blue Water Inn gets its name from it
- Winery (Wizard of Wines): Supply route for the wine; the Martikovs are very interested in what's happening there

## Atmosphere & Senses
- **Sound:** Enforced cheerfulness — hammering as another festival stage is constructed, music from the inn, and beneath it all a silence in the residential streets that speaks to people who have learned not to be overheard
- **Smell:** Pine smoke, fresh-baked bread from the market, and the sour-sweet smell of old wine from the inn; the stocks near the town square smell of rot and fear
- **Temperature:** Marginally warmer than the surrounding countryside by virtue of population density and the fires; the warmth feels conditional, like it could be revoked
