# Castle Ravenloft

## At a Glance
- **Type:** Gothic castle; seat of power; endgame dungeon
- **Region:** The Pillarstone of Ravenloft, above the village of Barovia; visible from nearly everywhere in the valley
- **Tone:** Sacred and ancient, beautiful and terrible — a place built by a great man that has become a monument to what he lost and what he became
- **Scale:** Vast — hundreds of rooms across multiple towers, bridges, courtyards, and subterranean levels; could take multiple sessions to explore
- **Controlled by:** Strahd von Zarovich

## Read-Aloud Description
The castle has been visible since you entered the valley — you have been traveling toward it without meaning to, the way a river travels toward a falls. Up close, the scale is staggering: the walls rise sheer from the rock of the promontory, towers clawing upward into the perpetual overcast, and the whole structure is carved from the same dark stone as the mountain itself, so that it is difficult to say where the rock ends and the castle begins. The architecture is old and confident — this was built by someone who knew exactly what they were doing, who had the money and the skill and the vision, and the result is genuinely beautiful in the way that only things built without compromise can be. The gates stand open. They usually do. Strahd has never needed a locked door to keep people in. Above the main entrance, carved in stone that was old when your grandparents were young, a name: RAVENLOFT. And from somewhere inside, so faint it might be your imagination, the sound of a harpsichord playing, the notes precise and cold and very, very good.

## DM-Only Notes
Strahd's tomb is in the catacombs beneath the castle — the lowest point of the dungeon, past crypts containing the remains of his family and ancestors and lieutenants. This is where he must be destroyed: the final confrontation requires reaching his coffin, driving him to it, and finishing him there. He cannot be permanently slain anywhere else.

The castle is enormous and does not need to be fully explored in one visit. The party will likely come here multiple times: once early (invited, possibly, or arriving with Ireena), once to gather information or retrieve an item, and finally for the endgame. Track what they've seen and what Strahd knows they know.

Strahd can be encountered almost anywhere in the castle. He may invite the party to dinner the first time they arrive. Play that dinner as the most civilized, terrifying social encounter of the campaign: excellent food, impeccable manners, complete honesty about the fact that he will not allow them to leave Barovia. He is not performing menace — he simply occupies a position so far above the party's current power level that he can afford to be gracious about it.

The castle contains: vampire spawn servants, suits of animated armor, gargoyles, a beholder (in the Amber Temple, or in some configurations here), rooms dedicated to Tatyana and each of her reincarnations (including Ireena), a fully stocked library of Strahd's centuries of magical research, an organ pipe system that runs through the walls, the coffin room with the three vampire brides, and at the very bottom, the tomb.

The heart mechanic: destroy Strahd, and the castle begins to fall. Give the party about five rounds to get out.

## Key Features
- **The Drawbridge and Main Gates:** Open; the entrance is easy; the exit is the problem
- **The Great Entry Hall:** Vaulted, torchlit, portraits of the von Zarovich lineage on every wall; Strahd's face in the most recent ones, unchanged
- **The Study:** Strahd's personal workspace; centuries of journals, correspondence, and magical notes; extraordinary information if the party can reach it
- **The Dining Hall:** Where Strahd may invite the party to dinner; long table, excellent wine, a host who will tell you almost anything if you ask the right questions
- **The Chapel:** Desecrated long ago; the ghost of a priest lingers; the architecture suggests what this place was before
- **The Catacombs:** Multi-level underground crypts; the ancestral dead of the Von Zarovich line; Strahd's tomb at the lowest point
- **The Tower Tops:** Views across all of Barovia; Strahd stands here sometimes, looking down at the valley he has ruled for four hundred years

## Occupants
- Strahd von Zarovich: Master of the castle and the domain; may be encountered at almost any time; treat every encounter as a choice he is making
- Rahadin: Chamberlain; runs the practical administration of the castle; first point of contact for most visitors
- Vampire spawn (various): Servants, guards, and former consorts; dangerous individually, more dangerous in groups
- The three vampire brides: Volenta, Ludmilla, and Anastrasya; each distinct in personality and tactics; not loyal to each other
- Animated armor and other constructs: The castle's passive defenses

## Connections
- Village of Barovia: Directly below; Strahd can reach the village in minutes
- Old Svalich Road: Visible from the battlements; every traveler on that road is visible to anyone watching from the towers
- The Amber Temple: The source of Strahd's original dark gift; not in the castle but connected to his history and power

## Atmosphere & Senses
- **Sound:** Wind through arrow slits, the distant sound of a harpsichord from somewhere above, the occasional clank of armored patrols, and a deep bass resonance in the stone itself — the mountain breathing, or something like it
- **Smell:** Cold stone, old torch smoke, dried flowers that were placed in vases decades ago and never replaced, and very faintly beneath everything, the copper-sweet smell of old blood baked into the walls over centuries
- **Temperature:** Cold and consistent — not the damp chill of the outdoors but the dry, stable cold of deep stone, the temperature of a place that has not been warm since its master stopped needing warmth
