# Ireena Kolyana

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Adopted daughter of the late Burgomaster Kolyan Indirovich; the current reincarnation of Tatyana; primary escort objective for the early campaign
- **Location:** Barovia village (starting); intended destination is St. Andral's Church in Vallaki
- **Stat Block:** Noble (MM p. 348) — AC 15 (breastplate), HP 9, rapier +3 to hit / 1d8+1 piercing
- **Faction:** None / Independent

## Appearance
She has auburn hair and striking green eyes that people sometimes find unsettling without knowing why — there is something behind them that feels older than her face. Two small puncture wounds are visible on the left side of her neck, partially concealed but not hidden; she stopped trying to cover them after her father died.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks directly and without hedging, more like a soldier delivering a report than a grieving daughter — this is how she holds herself together. When she is genuinely frightened she goes very quiet rather than louder.
- **Physical Tell:** Keeps her right hand near the hilt of her rapier when uncertain about a new person; only relaxes that hand when she decides to trust someone.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To bury her father with dignity and get out of Barovia village before Strahd comes for her again — she will not leave without Ismark.
- **Want (deep):** To protect the people she loves and eventually understand why she was chosen, why Strahd looks at her the way he does, and whether any of this was ever truly her choice.
- **Secret:** She has begun experiencing fragmented memory-flashes that don't belong to her — a stone bridge over a cliff, a red dress, a man with dark eyes who looked at her the way Strahd looks at her now. She doesn't understand them yet and hasn't told Ismark. She is starting to be afraid she is losing her mind.

## Relationships
- **Ismark Kolyanovich:** Her brother; she loves him fiercely and worries he will get himself killed trying to prove himself
- **Kolyan Indirovich (deceased):** Her adoptive father; his death has left a wound she hasn't had time to process
- **Strahd von Zarovich:** She knows he is hunting her; she refuses to be reduced to prey, but she cannot pretend she isn't frightened
- **Donavich (priest of Barovia):** She has complicated feelings about him — he failed the village while lost in grief over his son, but she does not entirely blame him

## Sample Dialogue
> "I am not going to pretend I don't know what those marks on my neck mean, or what he wants from me. I'd rather talk about what we're going to do about it."
> "My father is dead. Ismark needs me. I'm not going to fall apart in the street — I'll fall apart later, somewhere private. Right now I need to know if you're going to help us or not."
> "I keep dreaming about a bridge I've never seen. A woman in red. I don't... it's nothing. Forget I said anything."

## DM Notes
Ireena is the moral center of the early campaign and one of the party's most important early bonds. She should feel like a person with agency, not a package to be delivered. She will ask questions about the party, share her opinions, and push back if the party does something she finds wrong.

She is not naive about her situation — she has been bitten twice and watched Strahd's wolves circle her father's house for weeks. Her bravery is not ignorance, it is will. The party should admire her, not pity her.

The fragmented Tatyana memories are a slow-burn mystery to introduce carefully. Drop one image early (perhaps while she sleeps during a rest, and she wakes shaken), and let the party notice before she fully processes what it means. When she eventually learns the full truth of the reincarnation cycle, how she responds is a powerful character moment — she may feel violated, determined, grieving, or some combination.

Her arc resolves in Krezk at the blessed pool (Chapter 8), where she is reunited with Sergei's spirit — Tatyana's original love, not Strahd's — and peacefully departs the material world. This moment should feel earned. The party should understand they didn't fail; they gave her peace that Strahd never could.

Do not let NPCs or Strahd reduce her to a prize in front of the party without her pushing back. She is a person, and the most effective way to make Strahd's obsession feel monstrous is to make Ireena fully real first.
