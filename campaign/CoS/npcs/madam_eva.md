# Madam Eva

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Ancient Vistana seer; the reader of the Tarokka; the party's oracle for artifact and ally locations
- **Location:** Tser Pool Encampment (Chapter 2, Area G), north of Barovia village along the Old Svalich Road
- **Stat Block:** Druid (MM p. 346) as written; treat as archmage-equivalent in terms of narrative power and what she chooses not to use
- **Faction:** Vistani (independent — answers to no one, including Strahd)

## Appearance
She is impossibly old — deeply wrinkled, bent over a gnarled walking staff, with white hair worn loose and wild around her shoulders. Her dark eyes are sharp and quick in a way that makes the rest of her face seem like a mask she's borrowed; they track movement in a room before other people's eyes have adjusted. She is always found near firelight, which she seems to prefer to any lamp or torch.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks in a low, unhurried murmur, as if sharing a secret she has told ten thousand times before. She is cryptic not because she enjoys riddles but because she speaks in the language of pattern and symbol — literal statements feel clumsy to her. She sometimes finishes a visitor's sentence with an accuracy that feels wrong.
- **Physical Tell:** Holds the Tarokka deck in both hands even when she isn't reading it, the way another person might hold a cup of hot tea; it appears to comfort her, or ground her. She closes her eyes for a long moment before drawing each card.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To conduct the reading accurately. She takes this duty seriously — the reading is not performance, it is the most important thing she will do in any given year, and she will not rush it or soften it.
- **Want (deep):** Rest. She has been a vessel for Mother Night longer than any mortal should live, and she carries the accumulated grief of watching generation after generation of adventurers come to Barovia full of hope. She does not speak of this want, but it is there in the stillness behind her eyes.
- **Secret:** She is an avatar or vessel of Mother Night, the goddess of darkness who is one of the Dark Powers binding Barovia. She has conducted this same reading — in its essential shape, if not its specific results — for hundreds of years, for hundreds of parties who came to end Strahd's curse. She remembers all of them. She has watched all of them fail or succeed, seen what the Dark Powers do with both outcomes. She cannot tell the party this. It would break something in the fabric of the reading itself — or perhaps in her.

## Relationships
- **Strahd von Zarovich:** He does not command her and she does not fear him; he knows enough about what she is to leave her alone, which is its own kind of fear
- **The Vistani:** She is their eldest and their mystery — they revere her but do not truly know her, and she keeps it that way kindly
- **Mother Night:** She is not separate from this power so much as she is its current expression in the world; the boundary between Madam Eva the woman and Mother Night the goddess has become very thin
- **Previous adventuring parties (unnamed):** She carries them all — every face, every death, every near-victory; they are the weight behind her eyes

## Sample Dialogue
> "Sit. The cards have been waiting for you since before you crossed the mists. I did not say *patiently*."
> "You want me to tell you plainly what you face. I could. But you would not believe a plain telling. The cards give you what you need to believe — and belief is what survives when courage runs out."
> "I have seen this before. I have seen it many times. That does not mean it ends the same way. The cards are not destiny. They are *direction*."

## DM Notes
The Tarokka reading (Chapter 1) is one of the campaign's most important scenes and Madam Eva is its anchor. Run it with ceremony. Use physical cards if you have them (the Tarokka deck is sold separately). Dim the lights. Take your time.

Madam Eva should feel ancient in a way that is different from Strahd's age — Strahd is ancient in the way a predator is ancient, through accumulation of power and cruelty. Eva is ancient in the way a river is ancient: patient, deep, running on cycles the living can barely perceive. She is not warm exactly, but she is not cold either. She is simply *very far away*, even when she is right in front of you.

She will not answer direct questions about Strahd's weaknesses, the Dark Powers, or her own nature. She answers questions about the reading. Attempts to press her on anything else are met with a silence that does not feel rude — it feels like asking a mountain a question and waiting for an answer.

She is not a combat encounter under any normal circumstances. If the party is foolish enough to threaten her, have her look at them the way you would look at a child who has picked up a knife. She will not react with fear. Whether she would be harmed is a question the party should never get an answer to.

The secret of her identity as a Mother Night vessel is for the DM's table feel and long-term resonance, not necessarily something that gets revealed explicitly. But it should inform every choice she makes — she has seen too much to be surprised, and too much to be indifferent.
