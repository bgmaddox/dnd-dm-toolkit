# Ismark Kolyanovich

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Son of the late Burgomaster of Barovia village; Ireena's brother; de facto acting leader of the village pending formal succession
- **Location:** Barovia village, the Burgomaster's mansion
- **Stat Block:** Veteran (MM p. 350) — AC 17 (splint), HP 58, longsword +5 to hit / 1d8+3 slashing
- **Faction:** None

## Appearance
He is a broad-shouldered man in his late twenties with a fighter's build and dark circles under his eyes that have been there for months — he has not slept well since Strahd began circling the house. He keeps his dark hair cut short and his jaw shaved, a deliberate neatness that says he is trying to hold something together even when everything feels like it's falling apart.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks with the slightly too-formal cadence of someone who grew up hearing his father give speeches — it comes out when he is nervous or trying to project authority he isn't sure he has. With the party, once he trusts them, he drops the formality and becomes warmer and more direct.
- **Physical Tell:** Rubs the back of his neck with one hand when he's ashamed or doubting himself; stands very straight when others are watching, as if he knows they're comparing him to his father.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To bury his father properly and get Ireena safely to Vallaki before Strahd bites her a third time — he knows what a third bite means.
- **Want (deep):** To prove — to Barovia, to his father's memory, and to himself — that he is worth something. That "Ismark the Lesser" is not his final epitaph.
- **Secret:** He believes his father died because of him. Not because of anything he did, but because of what he failed to do — he didn't get Ireena out soon enough, and the months of stress and terror watching Strahd's creatures assault the house is what broke the old man's heart. He has never said this aloud to anyone, including Ireena.

## Relationships
- **Ireena Kolyana:** His adopted sister; he loves her completely and will die before he lets Strahd take her — this is not bravery exactly, it is a man with nothing left to lose
- **Kolyan Indirovich (deceased):** His father; the shadow of the old man's legacy sits on everything Ismark does
- **The village of Barovia:** He cares about the people here even as they whisper "the Lesser" behind his back; he wants to come back after Ireena is safe and do something that matters
- **Strahd von Zarovich:** Pure hatred, with enough self-knowledge to understand he is not in Strahd's weight class — this does not make the hatred smaller

## Sample Dialogue
> "They call me Ismark the Lesser. I've heard it. You can say it to my face if you want — it won't change what I'm asking you to do."
> "She's been bitten twice. If it happens again... I've heard what happens after a third bite. I'm not going to let that happen. I need your help to make sure of it."
> "When she's safe, I'm coming back. Barovia deserves better than a village full of people waiting to die. I don't know yet what I'm going to do about that. But I'm coming back."

## DM Notes
Ismark is the party's first meaningful ally and their main quest-giver out of the village. He should feel like a genuinely good man weighed down by grief, guilt, and a name he didn't choose.

The "Lesser" nickname is important to introduce organically — have a villager mutter it within earshot, or have a drunk at the Blood of the Vine tavern say it to his face. Let Ismark absorb it without reacting in the moment, then have him explain it to the party quietly later. This establishes his core wound without hitting it too hard too early.

He is not a coward. He has been holding the village together with his bare hands for months while Strahd's wolves tear at the house every night. The party should understand his bravery before they understand his shame.

His request is simple on the surface — escort Ireena to Vallaki — but the weight behind it is a brother who has already failed his father and cannot survive failing her too. If a player tries to comfort him or tells him his father's death wasn't his fault, he won't believe them, but it will mean something to him.

After the escort is complete, Ismark returns to Barovia village. If the party passes through again, it is worth a brief scene showing him doing something concrete for the village — organizing patrols, reinforcing doors, taking his father's place with more conviction. Let him have a small arc of his own, even if the party only sees pieces of it.
