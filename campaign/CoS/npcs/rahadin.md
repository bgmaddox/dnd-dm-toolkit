# Rahadin

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Chamberlain of Castle Ravenloft; Strahd's enforcer and most loyal servant
- **Location:** Castle Ravenloft; travels throughout Barovia on Strahd's business
- **Stat Block:** Rahadin (unique, champion-level)
- **Faction:** Strahd's Court

## Appearance
Rahadin is a lean, ageless dusk elf with sharp cheekbones, close-cropped dark hair, and eyes the color of deep slate — calm, unhurried, and utterly without warmth. He dresses in dark riding leathers and a long coat, and he moves with the quiet precision of a man who has never needed to hurry because nothing has ever escaped him.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks softly, almost gently — he never raises his voice, never threatens. He states facts. "Lord Strahd requires your cooperation" is both greeting and verdict.
- **Physical Tell:** A faint, nearly imperceptible tightening around the eyes when he is about to act. Anyone who has seen it once and survived does not forget it.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To carry out Strahd's current orders with perfect efficiency — whether that means escorting guests, delivering a message, or eliminating a problem.
- **Want (deep):** To remain indispensable to Strahd — the one constant in centuries of changing courts, consorts, and schemes. Rahadin's identity is entirely bound to his service.
- **Secret:** He ordered the massacre of the dusk elves of Barovia — his own people — on Strahd's command. He does not regret it. He does not think about it. The true horror is not that he suppresses the guilt: there is no guilt to suppress. The screams of thousands of slaughtered innocents follow him everywhere, audible to all, and he has simply learned to ignore them.

## Relationships
- Strahd von Zarovich: Master and the sole object of his loyalty — Rahadin would burn the world flat if Strahd asked
- Kasimir Velikov (dusk elf survivor): The last elder of the people Rahadin destroyed; Kasimir's hatred is bottomless and Rahadin is aware of it with complete indifference
- Vampire spawn of Ravenloft: Views them as tools, interchangeable and beneath consideration

## Sample Dialogue
> "Lord Strahd has taken an interest in your group. You should consider that a privilege."
> "I have no quarrel with you. That can change."
> "The screaming? You get used to it. I did."

## DM Notes
Rahadin is the face of Strahd's authority in the world below the castle. When Strahd wants to send a message without appearing himself, Rahadin arrives. He should feel like a force of nature — not monstrous in the way of a vampire spawn, but in the way of a perfected instrument. He is polished, courteous, and absolutely lethal.

The psychic scream aura is his most important atmospheric tool. Every character within 10 feet hears the distant wailing of thousands of dying people — it should be described every time Rahadin enters a scene, because it tells the party exactly what kind of man this is before he speaks a word.

Play him without malice. He does not enjoy causing pain; he simply does not weigh it. If the party tries to reason with him, he will listen politely and then do whatever Strahd ordered anyway. He is not a villain who can be redeemed through good argument. He is a philosophical endpoint — loyalty taken so far it has consumed everything else that was human in him.

He makes an excellent recurring presence: the party sees him early as an escort or messenger, understands he is dangerous, and then has to decide whether to confront him later knowing the cost. A fight with Rahadin should feel earned and costly.
