# Strahd von Zarovich

## At a Glance
- **Role:** Vampire lord, absolute ruler of Barovia, the Dark Power's prisoner and instrument
- **Location:** Castle Ravenloft (primary); may appear anywhere in Barovia at will
- **Stat Block:** Strahd von Zarovich (unique — see *Curse of Strahd* p. 239)
- **Faction:** Strahd's Court (leader)

## Appearance
He is tall, gaunt, and aristocratic, with pale skin stretched over sharp cheekbones and coal-black hair swept back from a widow's peak. His eyes are deep-set and dark, calm as a man who has not needed to hurry in four centuries — he wears the black and red of Ravenloft like a second skin, and his expression rests in a permanent, patient half-smile.

## Voice & Manner
- **Voice Quirk:** Speaks in long, unhurried sentences with an Eastern European cadence; never raises his voice — when he grows dangerous, he speaks *more softly*, not louder. He frequently answers a question with a question.
- **Physical Tell:** Clasps his hands behind his back when genuinely interested in someone; turns away and looks toward a window when bored or deciding whether someone lives.

## Motivations
- **Want (immediate):** To possess Ireena Kolyana — not merely as a trophy, but because he believes she is Tatyana returned, and he has waited 400 years for this moment.
- **Want (deep):** To escape the mists of Barovia and reclaim a life that has meaning — or, failing that, to find someone worthy enough to end it for him.
- **Secret:** He is exhausted. Four centuries of immortality have hollowed him out, and somewhere beneath the cruelty and the games he plays with adventurers, part of him wants the party to succeed — to find the weapons, to come for him, to finally force an end. He will never yield willingly, and he will deny this truth with his last breath, but it is the engine behind his strange generosity toward worthy enemies.

## Relationships
- **Ireena Kolyana:** The reincarnation of Tatyana, his obsession and the axis of his current plans
- **Rahadin:** His most loyal chamberlain and enforcer; one of the few creatures Strahd trusts
- **Madam Eva:** He neither controls nor fully understands her; he respects this, which is itself remarkable
- **The Dark Powers:** His jailers — he made a bargain at the Amber Temple and has been trapped in Barovia ever since
- **Tatyana (memory):** The human woman he loved and lost on the day of his transformation; every reincarnation reopens the wound

## Sample Dialogue
> "You've come a long way, and I confess — most who stand where you stand are already dead. That you are not tells me something about you. I haven't decided yet if it is something good."
> "She does not belong to you. She does not even belong to herself. But I have been patient before. I can be patient again."
> "You wish to destroy me. I find that... refreshing. Come to dinner. Come armed if it makes you feel better. We will talk, as civilized people should, before one of us does something we regret."

## DM Notes
Strahd should never feel like a dungeon boss waiting in a room — he is an active, intelligent presence who watches the party from the moment they enter Barovia. He may observe them for sessions before making contact. His first direct interaction should feel like an interview, not a confrontation.

Play him as a man who has had 400 years to master every social grace and every cruelty. He is genuinely curious about the party. He will compliment the fighter's technique, ask the cleric uncomfortable theological questions, and speak to the rogue like a peer. He is not performing charm — he *is* charming, which is what makes him dangerous.

He uses three modes depending on his read of the party: **Gracious Host** (dinner at Ravenloft, sending gifts, offering information freely), **Patient Hunter** (allowing them to gather the artifacts, because the chase amuses him and strong enemies are more satisfying to defeat), and **Merciless Lord** (when they threaten Ireena or genuinely wound his pride). Move between these modes as earned by the fiction.

He will not kill the party outright when he easily could — not because he is merciful, but because he is bored, and they are the most interesting thing to happen in Barovia in decades. The moment they stop being interesting, that protection evaporates.

The dinner at Ravenloft (Chapter 2) is the set piece to lean into: candlelight, impeccable wine, uncomfortable questions about the party's pasts, and a moment near the end where he looks at Ireena across the table with an expression the party will not be able to read as anything other than longing.
