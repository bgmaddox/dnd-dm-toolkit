# Vision & Design Principles

## Why This Exists

Running D&D well requires managing three things simultaneously: mechanics (rules, dice, stats),
narrative (story, pacing, consequences), and performance (NPC voices, descriptions, atmosphere).
Experienced DMs have internalized most of this. New DMs haven't — and the cognitive overload of
tracking all three kills immersion and spontaneity.

These tools are designed around one core idea: **reduce cognitive load so the DM can stay present
with the players.**

## Design Principles

### 1. Speed Over Depth at the Table
A tool used during live play must return useful output in under 30 seconds of total interaction.
If it takes longer, the DM breaks eye contact with the players and immersion suffers. Prefer
pre-generated content over live generation wherever possible.

### 2. Generative AI for Creative Gaps, Structured Data for Lookups
Claude is excellent at: NPC personalities, scene descriptions, improv responses, plot hooks,
dialogue samples. Claude is not needed for: monster stat blocks, spell descriptions, condition
rules — those should be fast structured lookups.

### 3. Campaign Context is Everything
Generic NPC generators and generic encounter builders exist everywhere. What doesn't exist is a
tool that knows *your* campaign — your NPCs, your factions, your party composition, your running
plot threads. Every tool should be buildable toward campaign-aware output.

### 4. Pre-session Prep Pays Off During Sessions
The best live-session tool is a well-prepped DM. Tools should encourage "banking" content before
the session: 5 NPCs prepped beats 0 prepped + hoping the generator works fast enough.

### 5. No Spoilers for Current Campaign
The DM is currently playing Lost Mines of Phandelver as a *player*. Do not expose plot details,
villain identities, or dungeon layouts for LMoP until the DM explicitly takes over and requests
that information.

## The Three Contexts

Tools are designed for one of three moments:

| Context | Time Available | Priority |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| **Session prep** (days before) | Hours | Depth, thoroughness, campaign integration |
| **Pre-session warmup** (30 min before) | 30 minutes | Quick review, banking emergency content |
| **Live at the table** | 10-30 seconds | Speed, minimal typing, output ready to use |

## What Success Looks Like

A session where:
- Every NPC the party talks to feels like a distinct person
- Scene descriptions take 20 seconds to deliver and land with the players
- When players do something unexpected, the DM has an idea within 15 seconds
- The DM is looking at the players more than the screen
